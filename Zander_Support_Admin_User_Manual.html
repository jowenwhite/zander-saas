Introduction

Welcome to Support Admin

Support Admin is your command center for managing the Zander platform. It gives you complete visibility into system health, customer accounts, and potential issues before they become problems.

What is Support Admin?

Think of Support Admin like the dashboard in your car. It tells you everything you need to know about how your system is running. Is the engine healthy? Are there any warning lights? How are your customers doing?

Support Admin answers all these questions in one place. No more guessing, no more surprises. You'll know exactly what's happening with your platform at all times.

Why Does This Matter?

When you can see problems early, you can fix them before customers notice. When you understand which customers are thriving and which might be struggling, you can take action to help them succeed.

Happy customers stay. Unhappy customers leave. Support Admin helps you keep more customers happy by giving you the insights to act proactively.

Who Uses Support Admin?

Support Admin is designed for:

  • Platform Administrators - Monitor system health and manage tenant accounts
  • Customer Success Teams - Identify at-risk customers and take proactive action
  • Technical Support - Diagnose issues and track system performance
  • Leadership - Get a bird's-eye view of platform health and customer status

Getting Started

Access Support Admin at: https://app.zanderos.com/admin/support-admin

You'll need administrator privileges to access this area.

Chapter 2

Health Tab

The Health Tab tells you if everything is working properly. It's the first place to check when you want to confirm the system is healthy.

The Four Status Cards

At the top of the Health Tab, you'll see four cards. Each one monitors a critical part of your system:

Card What It Monitors Why It Matters
API The main system that handles all requests If this is down, nothing works
Database Where all your data lives Slow database = slow everything
Email The email sending service Important for notifications and outreach
Stripe Payment processing system Critical for subscriptions and billing

Understanding the Colors

Each card shows a colored status indicator:

  • ● Green - Everything is working normally. No action needed.
  • ● Yellow - Something might be slow or degraded. Worth investigating.
  • ● Red - There's a problem that needs attention now.
ⓘ Auto-Refresh

The Health Tab updates automatically every 5 seconds. You'll always see the current status without refreshing the page.

The 7-Day History Charts

Below the status cards, you'll see charts showing the last 7 days of performance data. These charts help you spot patterns and trends.

For example, if response times are gradually increasing over the week, that might indicate a growing problem that needs investigation before it becomes critical.

What to Do If Something Is Red

If you see a red status card, here's what to do:

  1. Don't panic. Take note of which service is affected.
  2. Check the Diagnostics Tab for recent error logs related to that service.
  3. Look at the charts to see when the problem started.
  4. Check if users are affected by looking at recent support tickets or complaints.
  5. Escalate if needed by contacting the technical team with your findings.
💡 Pro Tip

Check the Health Tab first whenever users report slow performance. Nine times out of ten, the answer is right there in the status cards.

Tenants Tab

The Tenants Tab is where you manage all your customers. View their status, make changes, and keep everything organized from one central place.

Understanding the Tenant Table

The main view shows a table with all your tenants. Each row is one customer. Here's what each column means:

Column What It Shows
Company Name The customer's business name
Tier Subscription level (Starter, Pro, Business, Enterprise)
Status Active, Trial, or Archived
Engagement How actively they use the platform (0-100 score)
Churn Risk Low, Medium, High, or Critical risk of leaving
Last Activity When they last logged in or used the system

Finding a Tenant

Use the search and filter tools at the top of the table:

  • Search box - Type any part of the company name to find them instantly
  • Tier filter - Show only Starter, Pro, or Enterprise customers
  • Status filter - Show Active, Trial, or Archived tenants
  • Column sorting - Click any column header to sort by that field

What You Can Do With Each Tenant

Click the actions menu (three dots) on any tenant row to see available actions:

  • View Details - See everything about this tenant including users, activity, and billing
  • Rename - Change the company name
  • Change Tier - Upgrade or downgrade their subscription level
  • Extend Trial - Give them more time before the trial expires
  • Archive - Hide inactive customers while keeping their data safe
  • Restore - Bring back an archived tenant

Step-by-Step: How to Rename a Tenant

  1. Find the tenant in the table using search or scroll
  2. Click the three dots menu on their row
  3. Select "Rename" from the dropdown
  4. Type the new company name in the dialog
  5. Click "Save" to confirm

The change takes effect immediately and is logged in the Activity Log.

Step-by-Step: How to Extend a Trial

  1. Find the tenant who needs more trial time
  2. Click the three dots menu on their row
  3. Select "Extend Trial"
  4. Choose how many days to add: 7, 14, or 30 days
  5. Click "Extend" to confirm

The extension happens immediately. The tenant will see their new trial end date on their next login.

Multi-Select: Batch Actions

Need to do the same action to multiple tenants? Use multi-select. Check the boxes next to each tenant you want to modify, then use the batch actions toolbar that appears.

This is especially useful for archiving multiple inactive tenants at once or extending trials for a whole cohort.

⚠ Important

Archiving hides a tenant from your main view, but it keeps all their data safe. You can always restore an archived tenant later. No data is ever deleted by archiving.

Activity Log

Every change you make to a tenant is recorded in the Activity Log. Click "View Details" on any tenant, then scroll down to see the complete history of changes.

This is your audit trail. It shows who made each change, when, and what was changed. Perfect for accountability and troubleshooting.

Diagnostics Tab

The Diagnostics Tab helps you find and fix problems. It shows error logs, performance metrics, and trend charts so you can investigate issues quickly.

The Four Stat Cards

At the top, you'll see four key metrics that summarize system performance:

Metric What It Means Good Value
Uptime % How often the system is available 99.9% or higher
API Response Time How fast requests are processed Under 200ms
Database Latency How fast database queries run Under 50ms
Error Rate Percentage of requests that fail Under 0.1%

The Error Log Table

Below the stat cards, you'll see a table of recent errors. Each row represents something that went wrong in the system.

Understanding Error Levels

  • ERROR - Something failed. A request didn't complete successfully.
  • WARNING - Something unusual happened, but the request still worked.

Filtering Errors

Use the filter toolbar to narrow down what you're looking at:

  • Level filter - Show only errors, only warnings, or both
  • Tenant filter - See errors for a specific customer
  • Status code filter - Find specific HTTP error codes (400, 500, etc.)
  • Time range - Look at errors from the last hour, day, or week

Viewing Error Details

Click any error row to expand it and see the full details including the stack trace, request URL, and any additional metadata that can help you diagnose the problem.

ⓘ Auto-Refresh

The Diagnostics Tab updates every 30 seconds. New errors appear automatically without refreshing the page.

The 7-Day Trend Charts

The charts show historical data to help you spot patterns:

  • Response Time Trend - Two lines showing API and database response times over time
  • Error Rate Trend - An area chart showing how error rates change through the week
  • Service Uptime Bars - A bar chart showing uptime for each service by day

Look for patterns. Are errors higher on certain days? Does response time spike at specific hours? These patterns can reveal underlying issues.

What to Do If Error Rate Is High

  1. Check the time range - When did errors start increasing?
  2. Look at error details - Click the most recent errors to see what's failing
  3. Check if it's tenant-specific - Filter by tenant to see if one account is causing issues
  4. Cross-reference with Health Tab - Is a service showing yellow or red?
  5. Document your findings before escalating to the technical team
💡 Pro Tip

High error rates usually mean one of three things: a database connection issue, an API overload, or a third-party service outage. Check the Health Tab to narrow it down quickly.

Users Tab

The Users Tab helps you understand how your customers are doing. It groups tenants into segments based on their engagement, so you can take the right action at the right time.

The Three Segments

Tenants are automatically grouped into three segments based on their behavior. Each segment tells a different story:

At-Risk
What it means: These tenants haven't been active in 2-4 weeks. They might be losing interest.

What to do: Reach out with a friendly check-in. Ask if they need help. A small nudge can bring them back.
Power Users
What it means: These tenants are very active. They use lots of features and log in frequently. They're happy.

What to do: Celebrate them! Offer upgrades, ask for feedback, or invite them to be references.
Churning
What it means: These tenants haven't been active in 4+ weeks. They're at serious risk of canceling.

What to do: Act fast! Win-back campaigns, exit surveys, or personal outreach might save them.

Segment Tables

Each segment has its own table showing the tenants in that group. You'll see their company name, engagement score (0-100), days since last activity, and other relevant metrics.

Higher engagement scores are better. Think of it like a health score for the customer relationship.

Quick Actions

Each tenant in the segments has action buttons you can click to take immediate action. The available actions depend on which segment they're in:

Segment Available Actions
At-Risk Send reactivation email, Offer discount, Extend trial
Power Users Send upgrade offer, Schedule demo, Ask for feedback
Churning Win-back campaign, Exit survey, Check support tickets

Understanding "Draft" vs "Execute"

Some actions create drafts, others execute immediately. Here's the difference:

  • Draft actions (emails, campaigns) - Create a message for you to review before sending. Nothing goes out without your approval.
  • Execute actions (extend trial) - Happen immediately. The change takes effect right away.

You'll always see a confirmation dialog before any action, so there are no surprises.

Step-by-Step: Send a Reactivation Email

  1. Open the Users Tab
  2. Find the tenant in the At-Risk segment
  3. Click the "Actions" button on their row
  4. Select "Send reactivation email"
  5. Review the email draft that appears in the dialog
  6. Customize the message if needed
  7. Click "Create Draft" to save it for review, or "Send" if approved

Step-by-Step: Offer an Upgrade to a Power User

  1. Open the Users Tab
  2. Find the tenant in the Power Users segment
  3. Click the "Actions" button on their row
  4. Select "Send upgrade offer"
  5. Review the upgrade offer email draft
  6. Click "Create Draft" to save for review
💡 Pro Tip

Check the Users Tab weekly. Early action with at-risk customers prevents churn. A simple check-in email can make all the difference.

Zander Workflows

Zander works for you 24/7. While you sleep, Zander is analyzing your customers, spotting opportunities, and drafting communications for your review.

How It Works

Every night at 11 PM, Zander runs four automated workflows. Each one looks at your customer data and identifies opportunities for action. In the morning at 7 AM, Zander sends you a briefing with everything you need to know.

Important: Zander creates drafts, but never sends without your approval. You're always in control.

The Four Nightly Workflows

🔍 Churn Risk Mitigation

Zander finds tenants showing signs of disengagement. For each one, Zander drafts a personalized check-in email. You review and approve before anything goes out.

⭐ Power User Upgrades

Zander spots highly engaged users who might be ready for more. Zander drafts upgrade offers highlighting benefits relevant to their usage. You decide if and when to send.

⏰ Trial Expiration Warnings

When trials are about to expire (3 days or less), Zander creates alerts and drafts conversion emails. You'll never miss a trial deadline again.

♥ System Health Alerts

Zander monitors error rates, response times, and critical churn risks. If something looks concerning, you'll see an alert in your morning briefing.

Morning Briefing

Every morning at 7 AM, Zander compiles a summary of what happened overnight:

  • How many drafts are waiting for your review
  • Any new signups or waitlist entries
  • Critical alerts that need attention
  • Summary of workflow results (how many tenants processed, drafts created)

This briefing is stored in the system and accessible from the Support Admin dashboard. Start your day by reviewing it before your first meeting.

Workflow Schedule

☼ 11:00 PM EST All four workflows run
☀ 7:00 AM EST Morning briefing delivered

The Golden Rule: No Auto-Sending

Zander is smart, but you're the boss. All emails and communications created by Zander are saved as drafts. They wait for your approval before sending.

This means:

  • You can review and edit every message
  • You can discard drafts you don't want to send
  • You maintain complete control over customer communication
  • Zander handles the research and writing; you handle the decision
💡 Pro Tip

Review the morning briefing before your first meeting. It's usually 3-5 quick decisions: approve this draft, extend that trial, check on this alert. Takes 5 minutes, saves hours.

Common Questions

Q: What does "engagement score" mean?

A: It's a measure of how actively a tenant uses the system, from 0 to 100. 0-50 is low engagement, 50-75 is good, 75+ is excellent. It factors in logins, feature usage, and recent activity.

Q: Can I undo an archive?

A: Yes! Click "Restore" on any archived tenant to bring them back. All their data is preserved. Archiving is not deletion.

Q: How often do the charts update?

A: Health Tab updates every 5 seconds. Diagnostics Tab updates every 30 seconds. Both auto-refresh without you needing to reload the page.

Q: What's the difference between a draft and executing?

A: Drafts are created but not sent until you approve them. Executing (like extending a trial) happens immediately. You'll always see a confirmation dialog before any action.

Q: Can Zander send emails without my approval?

A: No. All emails created by Zander workflows are saved as drafts. They wait for your review and approval before sending. You're always in control.

Q: What if I see lots of errors in the Diagnostics Tab?

A: First, check when they started (look at the time range). Then check if it's tenant-specific (use the filter). Finally, cross-reference with the Health Tab to see if a service is down. Document your findings before escalating.
