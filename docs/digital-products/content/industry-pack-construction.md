## Chapter 1: The Construction Business Operating System

### The 5 Pillars of a Construction Business

Every successful contractor operates on these pillars:

```
                    REVENUE
                       ▲
                       │
    ┌──────────────────┼──────────────────┐
    │                  │                  │
ESTIMATING ────► PROJECT MGMT ◄──── OPERATIONS
    │                  │                  │
    └──────────────────┼──────────────────┘
                       │
                       ▼
                   FINANCE
```

**Estimating:** Win profitable work, not just any work
**Project Management:** Deliver on time, on budget
**Operations:** Build efficiently with skilled teams
**Finance:** Know your numbers, protect your margins
**Revenue:** Grow sustainably with the right clients

### The Construction Revenue Formula

```
Revenue = (Bid Volume × Win Rate × Avg Job Size)

Example:
  20 bids/month × 25% win rate × $50,000 avg = $250,000/month

To grow revenue, improve:
1. Bid Volume → Better marketing, more relationships
2. Win Rate → Better estimating, right-sizing bids
3. Average Job Size → Move upmarket, bundles
```

## Chapter 8: Growth and Scaling Playbook

### The Growth Decision Tree

```
ARE YOU READY TO GROW?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Hitting target margins on current jobs (>20% gross)
□ Turning away work regularly
□ Core team stable and reliable
□ Systems documented and repeatable
□ Cash reserves for growth investment
□ Clear on what growth looks like

GROWTH OPTIONS

Option 1: Higher Margins (Same Volume)
• Target more profitable work
• Reduce callbacks and rework
• Negotiate better sub/material pricing
• Improve estimating accuracy

Option 2: More Volume (Same Size Jobs)
• Add crews/crews
• Improve marketing
• Expand geographic area
• Shorten job cycle time

Option 3: Bigger Jobs
• Build larger project capabilities
• Add commercial or public work
• Develop specialization
• Pursue design-build

Option 4: New Services
• Add complementary trades
• Offer maintenance/service
• Expand to new market segment
```
