### Cash Flow Rules

**Rule 1: Know Your Cash Cycle**
```
Days to Get Paid (Receivables)         _____ days
+ Days of Inventory on Hand            _____ days
- Days to Pay Suppliers (Payables)     _____ days
= Cash Conversion Cycle                _____ days
```

**Rule 2: The 3-Month Reserve**
Keep 3 months of operating expenses in cash at all times.

**Rule 3: Invoice Immediately**
Revenue isn't real until it's collected. Invoice the same day work is completed.

**Rule 4: Manage Payables Strategically**
- Pay early for discounts (2/10 net 30 = 36% annual return)
- Use full terms when cash is tight
- Negotiate longer terms with vendors

### Improving Cash Flow

**Speed Up Collections:**
- Invoice immediately
- Offer early payment discounts
- Accept credit cards
- Send reminder before due date
- Follow up on day 1 of being late
- Consider factoring for large receivables

**Slow Down Payments (Strategically):**
- Use full payment terms
- Negotiate 45 or 60-day terms
- Time large purchases carefully
- Use credit cards with grace periods

**Reduce Cash Tied Up:**
- Minimize inventory
- Require deposits on large orders
- Subscription/recurring revenue models
- Renegotiate fixed costs

## Chapter 4: Pricing Strategy

### The Pricing Framework

**Cost-Based Pricing**
```
Direct Costs per Unit               $______
+ Overhead Allocation               $______
= Total Cost per Unit               $______
× Markup (e.g., 1.5x)              ______
= Selling Price                     $______
```

**Value-Based Pricing**
```
Customer's Alternative Cost         $______
or Customer's Gain from Your Solution  $______
× Value Capture (10-30%)            ______
= Your Price                        $______
```

**Competitive Pricing**
```
Competitor A Price                  $______
Competitor B Price                  $______
Market Average                      $______
Your Differentiation Value          $______
= Your Price                        $______
```

### Pricing Psychology

| Technique | Example | Why It Works |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Charm pricing | $99 vs $100 | Left-digit bias |
| Anchor pricing | ~~$500~~ $299 | Anchoring effect |
| Tiered pricing | Good/Better/Best | Choice architecture |
| Bundle pricing | $200 separately, $150 together | Perceived value |
| Decoy pricing | Three tiers, middle looks best | Asymmetric dominance |

### Price Increase Playbook

**When to raise prices:**
- Costs have increased
- Demand exceeds capacity
- You're below market
- You've added value
- It's been more than 12 months

**How to communicate:**


## Chapter 7: Tax Planning Basics

### Tax Calendar (US)

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| Jan 15 | Q4 estimated taxes due |
| Jan 31 | W-2s, 1099s to employees/contractors |
| Mar 15 | S-Corp/Partnership returns due |
| Apr 15 | Individual/C-Corp returns due; Q1 estimated |
| Jun 15 | Q2 estimated taxes due |
| Sep 15 | Q3 estimated taxes due; Extended returns due |

### Tax Reduction Strategies

**Entity Structure:**
- S-Corp election can reduce self-employment tax
- Reasonable salary required, but profits pass through

**Retirement Accounts:**
- Solo 401(k): Up to $66,000 (2024)
- SEP-IRA: Up to 25% of compensation
- SIMPLE IRA: Up to $16,000 + employer match

**Deductions:**
- Home office (dedicated space required)
- Vehicle (actual expenses or standard mileage)
- Business meals (50% deductible)
- Professional development
- Health insurance (self-employed deduction)

### Working with an Accountant

What to expect:
- **Monthly/Quarterly:** Bookkeeping review, estimated taxes
- **Year-End:** Tax planning strategy session
- **Tax Filing:** Preparation and submission
- **Ad Hoc:** Advice on major decisions

What to prepare for meetings:
- Bank and credit card statements
- Invoices and receipts
- Payroll reports
- Loan statements
- Questions prepared in advance

### Financial Red Flags

Watch for these warning signs:

**Cash Flow:**
- Consistently negative operating cash flow
- Relying on credit to make payroll
- Receivables growing faster than revenue

**Profitability:**
- Declining gross margin
- Operating expenses growing faster than revenue
- Negative EBITDA

**Balance Sheet:**
- Current ratio below 1.0
- Increasing debt without corresponding growth
- Customer concentration >30%
