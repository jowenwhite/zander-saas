## Chapter 1: Building Your Hiring Foundation

### Before You Hire: The Position Audit

Before posting any job, answer these questions:

**Is this hire necessary?**
- Can existing team members cover this work?
- Can we outsource or automate instead?
- What happens if we don't hire?

**What does success look like?**
- What will this person accomplish in 90 days?
- What will they own in 1 year?
- How will we measure their performance?

**What's the real budget impact?**
```

Recruiting costs: $_____
```

### Compensation Philosophy

Decide your approach before you start:

| Approach | Description | Best For |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Above Market | Pay 75th percentile+ | High-growth, competitive roles |
| At Market | Pay 50th percentile | Most roles, stable growth |
| Below Market + Equity | Lower base, ownership stake | Startups, believers in mission |
| Performance Heavy | Lower base, high variable | Sales, revenue-driving roles |

## Chapter 2: Job Description Templates

### The Job Description Framework

Every job description should include:

1. **Hook** - Why this role matters (2-3 sentences)
2. **About Us** - Company context (keep it short)
3. **What You'll Do** - 5-8 key responsibilities
4. **What You Bring** - Requirements (musts vs. nice-to-haves)
5. **What We Offer** - Compensation and benefits
6. **How to Apply** - Clear next step

### Sample: Operations Manager

```
OPERATIONS MANAGER
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

YOUR IMPACT
We're looking for an Operations Manager who will be the
backbone of our growing team. You'll build the systems
that let us scale while maintaining the quality our
customers expect.

ABOUT [COMPANY]
[2-3 sentences about company, mission, and stage]

WHAT YOU'LL DO
• Design and optimize operational processes across the
  company, reducing friction and increasing efficiency
• Manage vendor relationships and negotiate contracts
  to improve terms and reduce costs by 15%+
• Build and maintain documentation for all core processes,
  creating our operational playbook
• Lead weekly operations review, identifying bottlenecks
  and implementing solutions
• Manage office operations, facilities, and equipment
• Support hiring, onboarding, and HR administration
• Own key metrics dashboard and report to leadership

WHAT YOU BRING
Required:
• 3+ years in operations, project management, or similar
• Track record of building processes from scratch
• Strong analytical skills with Excel/Google Sheets
• Excellent written and verbal communication
• Experience managing vendors or external partners

Nice to Have:
• Experience in [industry] or similar fast-paced environment
• Familiarity with tools like Notion, Asana, or Monday.com
• PMP or other relevant certification

WHAT WE OFFER
• Competitive salary: $[X] - $[Y] based on experience
• Health, dental, and vision insurance
• 401(k) with [X]% company match
• Unlimited PTO (with minimum usage requirement)
• Professional development budget
• Flexible work arrangements

HOW TO APPLY
Send your resume and a brief note explaining why you're
interested to [email]. In your note, please share an
example of a process you built or improved.
```

```
SALES REPRESENTATIVE

• Strong phone presence and communication skills

Nice to Have:

"Sales Rep - [Your Name]". Include your quota performance
from your last 2 roles (% to target).
```

## Chapter 3: The Interview System

### The Structured Interview Process

Consistency is key. Every candidate should experience:

```
INTERVIEW STAGES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stage 1: Phone Screen (20-30 min)
  - Basic qualifications
  - Salary expectations
  - Timeline and logistics
  - Cultural fit indicators

Stage 2: Skills Assessment (30-60 min)
  - Role-specific test or case study
  - Technical skills verification
  - Problem-solving approach

Stage 3: Team Interview (45-60 min)
  - Deeper dive with hiring manager
  - Cross-functional team members
  - Culture fit assessment

Stage 4: Final Interview (30-45 min)
  - Executive/senior leadership
  - Final questions and concerns
  - Sell the opportunity

Reference Checks (after verbal offer acceptance)
  - 2-3 professional references
  - Verify claims and fit
```

### Phone Screen Questions

```
PHONE SCREEN SCRIPT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Opening:
"Thanks for taking the time today. This is an initial
conversation to learn more about your background and share
about the role. I have about 20 minutes—does that work?"

Questions:
1. Walk me through your background and how you got to
   where you are today.

2. What attracted you to this opportunity?

3. Tell me about your current/most recent role. What
   were your primary responsibilities?

4. What are you looking for in your next position?

5. What are your salary expectations for this role?

6. What's your timeline? Are you interviewing elsewhere?

7. What questions do you have about the role or company?

Close:
"Thanks for sharing. Here's what happens next:
[Explain next steps and timeline]"
```

### Behavioral Interview Questions

Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result

**Leadership/Management:**
- Tell me about a time you had to deliver difficult feedback.
- Describe a situation where you motivated a struggling team member.
- Give an example of a decision you made that was unpopular.

**Problem-Solving:**
- Walk me through how you approached a complex problem.
- Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
- Describe a project that didn't go as planned. What happened?

**Collaboration:**
- Give an example of a successful cross-functional project you led.
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague. How did you resolve it?
- Describe how you've built relationships with stakeholders.

**Results/Achievement:**
- What's the accomplishment you're most proud of?
- Tell me about a time you exceeded expectations.
- Describe a goal you set and how you achieved it.

### Scoring Rubric

Rate candidates consistently:

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Exceptional - Exceeds all requirements, immediate impact |
| 4 | Strong - Meets all requirements, some exceed |
| 3 | Acceptable - Meets most requirements |
| 2 | Weak - Missing key requirements |
| 1 | Poor - Does not meet requirements |

## Chapter 4: Onboarding Checklists

### Pre-Start Checklist (HR/Manager)

**1 Week Before Start:**
```
□ Offer letter signed and filed
□ Background check complete (if applicable)
□ Equipment ordered and configured
□ Accounts created (email, systems, etc.)
□ Workspace prepared
□ Onboarding schedule created
□ Welcome announcement drafted
□ Day 1 buddy assigned
□ First week meetings scheduled
```

### Day 1 Checklist

```
EMPLOYEE DAY 1
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Morning:
□ Greeted at arrival by manager or buddy
□ Workspace tour and introductions
□ Equipment and access confirmed
□ HR paperwork completed (I-9, W-4, benefits enrollment)
□ Emergency contact and policies reviewed

Afternoon:
□ 1:1 with direct manager
   - Role overview and expectations
   - 90-day goals discussion
   - Communication preferences
□ Meet key team members
□ Review company handbook
□ Access all needed systems and tools
□ End-of-day check-in
```

### First Week Checklist

```
WEEK 1 ONBOARDING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Day 2:
□ Deep dive on company: history, mission, values
□ Product/service overview
□ Shadow team members
□ Begin training modules

Day 3:
□ Meet cross-functional partners
□ Review key processes and documentation
□ Continue training
□ First small assignment

Day 4:
□ Customer overview (who we serve, why)
□ Tools and systems training
□ Progress check with manager

Day 5:
□ Week 1 review with manager
□ Clarify questions and concerns
□ Set Week 2 priorities
□ Feedback on onboarding experience
```

## Chapter 6: Team Culture Building

### Defining Your Culture

Culture isn't ping pong tables and free snacks. It's how people behave when no one's watching.

**The Culture Canvas:**

```
CULTURE DEFINITION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Our Mission: _______________

Our Core Values:
1. _______________ (Example behavior: _______________)
2. _______________ (Example behavior: _______________)
3. _______________ (Example behavior: _______________)

We hire people who: _______________

We don't hire people who: _______________

The best thing about working here: _______________

What outsiders don't understand: _______________
```

### Team Rituals

Build connection through consistent practices:

**Daily:**
- Morning standup (15 min max)
- End-of-day wins channel

**Weekly:**
- Team meeting with rotating facilitator
- Learning share (someone teaches something)
- Friday wins celebration

**Monthly:**
- All-hands meeting
- Social event (optional, but offered)
- Birthday/milestone recognition

**Quarterly:**
- Offsite or team building
- Strategy review
- Individual career conversations

**Annually:**
- Company retreat
- Annual review and goal setting
- Service day or volunteering

### Recognition Programs

**Peer Recognition:**
- Slack channel for shoutouts
- Monthly peer-nominated awards
- Value-based recognition cards

**Manager Recognition:**
- Spot bonuses for exceptional work
- Public acknowledgment in meetings
- Written notes of appreciation

**Company Recognition:**
- Quarterly top performer awards
- Anniversary celebrations
- Promotion announcements

## Chapter 7: Retention Strategies

### Why People Leave

Exit interview data consistently shows:

1. **Manager relationship** (most common)
2. **Career growth** - No clear path forward
3. **Compensation** - Fell behind market
4. **Work-life balance** - Burnout
5. **Culture fit** - Values misalignment

### Retention Levers

**Career Development:**
- Clear advancement paths
- Regular career conversations
- Learning and development budget
- Stretch assignments
- Mentorship programs

**Compensation:**
- Annual market analysis
- Merit increases tied to performance
- Equity refresh (if applicable)
- Retention bonuses for key roles

**Work Environment:**
- Flexible work options
- Manager training
- Workload management
- Team health monitoring

**Engagement:**
- Regular feedback loops
- Recognition programs
- Social connection opportunities
- Involvement in decisions

### Stay Interview Questions

Don't wait for exit interviews. Ask current employees:

1. What do you look forward to at work?
2. What's one thing that might tempt you to leave?
3. What talent do you have that we're not using?
4. What would you change about your job?
5. When was the last time you thought about leaving? What caused it?

## Chapter 8: HR Compliance Essentials

### Employment Law Checklist

**At Hire:**
```
□ I-9 completed within 3 days
□ W-4 completed
□ State tax forms if applicable
□ Employee handbook acknowledgment
□ At-will employment acknowledgment
□ Non-compete/NDA if applicable
□ Direct deposit authorization
□ Emergency contact form
```

**Ongoing:**
```
□ Wage and hour compliance (overtime, breaks)
□ Anti-discrimination policies
□ Harassment prevention training
□ Safety training (OSHA compliance)
□ Workers' compensation coverage
□ Unemployment insurance
□ ADA accommodations process
□ FMLA tracking if applicable (50+ employees)
```

**At Termination:**
```
□ Final paycheck timing (check state law)
□ Benefits continuation (COBRA)
□ Exit interview conducted
□ Equipment returned
□ Access revoked
□ Reference policy communicated
□ Unemployment claim preparation
```

### Required Postings

Display in workplace:
- Federal minimum wage
- OSHA workplace safety
- EEO is the Law
- FMLA (if applicable)
- State-specific postings

### Documentation Best Practices

**Always document:**
- Performance issues and conversations
- Accommodations requests and responses
- Complaints and investigations
- Policy violations and consequences
- Positive recognition too

**Keep records for:**
- I-9: 3 years after hire or 1 year after termination
- Personnel files: 7 years after termination
- Payroll records: 7 years
- Benefits records: 6 years
